Aerospace Conference Aviation Medicine Exploration

Case No. 006 · Dermatology · Slogan Competition

World Leprosy Day'26- Slogan Writing

January 30, 2026 · Department of Dermatology, GMC Omandurar

Dermatology First Place Slogan Advocacy

As part of a World Leprosy Day initiative, I secured first place in a slogan writing competition with "See the art, not the stigma." The idea was deeply personal- I built the visual around a dancer's hand holding a lotus, reworked to resemble a hand affected by leprosy. The intention was to shift the lens: to see what is often marked as damage instead as form, story, and resilience.

Presented against that image, the slogan wasn't just a line but a perspective- that the visible marks left by disease don't have to be hidden or reduced to stigma, but can be reclaimed with dignity. In many ways, it drew from the same spirit seen in communities that redefine visible difference on their own terms, turning what was once seen as limitation into expression.

Receiving the award from the Dean and later engaging directly with individuals living with leprosy- distributing gifts and interacting beyond the stage- added weight to the moment. Being featured on my college's platform captured the recognition, but what stayed with me was the intent behind it: not just to raise awareness, but to quietly challenge how we choose to see.

Case No. 007 · Forensic Medicine · Quiz

Forensic Decode- Inter-college Quiz

March 25, 2026 · Government Stanley Medical College, Chennai

Forensic Medicine Quiz Inter-college Team Event

I participated in Forensic Decode, an inter-college forensic medicine quiz hosted by Stanley Medical College, as part of a two-member team. While we didn't make it past the prelims, the experience turned out to be far more valuable than the outcome.

Preparing for the quiz introduced me to a different way of engaging with the subject- figuring out how I read, retain, and recall information under pressure, and understanding the level of depth and consistency such competitions demand. It also marked my first exposure to a competitive quiz setting, where the pace, difficulty, and presence of participants across batches made it both challenging and unexpectedly exhilarating.

More than anything, it shifted how I approach preparation- not just to know, but to think quickly and apply. It's an experience I would return to without hesitation.
